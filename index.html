About Me

My name is Robert Alexander Rudicel, I professionally go by Alex, and eveyone calls me Rudi.
I do not mind sharing a nickname with one of the greatest football movies to ever exist,
Rudy, but the problem arises when my uncle, father, brother and I are in a room and someone shouts, "Hey Rudi!".
Regardless of my name issues, I have made the name Rudi into my own creation: a well rounded individual with a variety of skills both on and off the computer.
In May of 2019, I graduated from Wabash College with a Bachelor's Degree in English and Minors in Religion and Computer Science.
Computer Science was not offered as a Major at the college until the Fall 2019 semseter, which is why I am not a CS Major.
Many ask why I decided to go to Wabash if I knew I wanted to get into technology and there was no CS Major.
I tell everyone who asks these two things:


  • The other CS Minors and I knew this lack of a Major was an issue and with the help of the CS Department (of 3 professors),
    we were able to meet with potential candidates and provide feedback on who would become the fourth professor and thus enable the department to have a Major.
    As I have mentioned above, the college is now offering a CS Major meaning that we were succesful in finding and getting another professor hired for the college.
    I played an important role in shaping the future of the college as well as giving another option to incomming freshman for years to come.
    That is a really good feeling and makes me smile every time I think about it.
  • The other thing I tell people is that Wabash is a college founded on tradition.
    I was a third generation student at the college meaning that both my grandfather and father attended as well.
    While this is a neat aspect, the real reason I chose Wabash over other schools was the small size (< 100 students),
    the fantastic liberal arts education (that has made me well-rounded), and the fact that it was impossible to sit in the back of the class and just coast by.
    Professors knew when you were not in class and sometimes they would call you or even come to your room to see what the problem was.
    That was somenthing special that I knew I would be hard-pressed to find anywhere else.


While my education gave me many valuable resources such as critical thinking, problem solving, and effective time management, I still had trouble finding a job.
Most employers would see that I majored in English and that was the end of my application. Even though I have a very important skill due to my unique degree,
which is being able to translate from computer jargon into Layman's terms, that was still not enough for most employers to even consider giving me an interview.
So I turned to ElevenFifty for help and enrolled in the JavaScript class for August 2019.
While not garunteed employment after graduation, I know that with my desire to find a job combined with the tangible proof that I am more than a self-taught coder,
I will be able to get a job that I am really excited for. Finally, I know this is like a novel, but I did say I majored in English, right?

TL;DR- My name is Rudi, recent college grad in English, hoping to find a great job post-ElevenFifty.
